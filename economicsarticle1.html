Local and community wealth is decreasing



Local communities are rapidly decreasing their wealth and local businesses are failing.

We are actively transferring our wealth to large businesses and corporations. This is a modern problem in the global trade.

I wanted to mention this idea, because I have seen many small businesses go bankrupt or stop, since it was obvious the market wasn't sustainable.

What I am discussing is not negative blood between countries. But I will explain how supporting local businesses will result in higher local wealth. Whereas supporting non-local businesses results in a decrease in local wealth.

Western countries have been trading internationally for centuries and is one of the main reasons (later alongside the industrial revolution) that made them so powerful and wealthy in the first place (reference 1, reference 2). However, this also created wealth inequality, like the VOC. The Dutch became very wealthy, although Indonesians stayed relatively poor.

Now trade is becoming more equal. Products from Asia are still cheap. But when Asia's wealth goes up, product prices will also rise.

The problem I am sketching lies in community driven wealth. e.g. buying milk from the milkman, instead of a large supermarket chain. So we're basically failing our local businesses. (More on this down below)

More European- and Americans are buying Asian cars. What happens is that Western car brands get a smaller financial annual return. In other words, the Western car brands are slowly losing power in the market.

If we buy more products from another continent, Western countries will face wealth depression. Money goes out of the region, but it's not coming back. Again in other words, we are actively transferring our wealth to another region! You could view it as buying a carpet from a big chain, instead of your friend who crafts carpets with his hands.
Yes, it might cost more, but the wealth stays inside a group or circle, as it may.

We are failing our local businesses by ignoring their products. This results in higher prices for not even that better a product.
The same happened with wool products. Synthetic clothing is way cheaper, which cascaded into nobody buying wool (that is somewhat more expensive, but also more durable), slowing wool productions and spiralling into wool products becoming incredibly expensive.
